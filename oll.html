Last layer orientation

How to do it

  1. We still hold the cube so that white faces down.
  2. We have to find a corner on the bottom layer (the corner will contain yellow) that isn’t yet correctly rotated.
  3. After we find this corner we still keep white at the top and we rotate the cube (or move the bottom layer) so that our corner is on our right side. After that we perform the same algorithm as in stage 1 of the 2x2x2 LBL method (first layer). Algorithm: R U R’ U’. We do it until our corner we be rotated correctly.
  4. By now our cube looks scrambled but don’t worry about it, it is totally normal and if you will do everything correctly it will get solved.
  5. Next we have to find another corner on the bottom layer that isn’t correctly orientated. After we find it we have to move it underneath the place were we rotated our previous corner by rotating the bottom layer. It is very important that we do the algorithm in the same place and just move the corners underneath it, otherwise our cube will get scrambled and we will have to start over again.
  6. We continue doing that until all the corners are correctly orientated – THIS GIVES AN ORIENTATED SECOND LAYER!!!

    7. In the above example we want to correctly rotate the corner on the bottom layer (containing yellow). To do this we hold the cube so that white is on the top and green faces us (we must have the corner to rotate on our right). We do the R U R’ U’ algorithm twice, after this our corner is correctly rotated.
