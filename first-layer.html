First Layer

How to do it:

  1. We have to find any piece containing white on it.
  2. We rotate the whole cube so that white on our piece faces down.
  3. We then have to look on the upper layer for another piece containing white (if you can’t find one go to step 9).
  4. We check if the piece that we just found has any colors that are the same as on the first piece (apart from white)
  5. If it contains the same color (apart from white), we move the piece above its correct place (the place we want to insert it in). E.g. In the example below the red-blue-white (white can’t be seen) piece has to be inserted into the place labelled “Correct place” because both pieces have red:
  6. We carry out the R U R’ U’ algorithm in the place where we want to insert our element – we have to hold it on our right side (in this case we keep the red face at the front and perform the algorithm on the face where the element’s intended place is) until the edge is inserted and at the same time properly rotated (the red part of our edge has to touch the red face and the white part has to touch the white face):
  7. If it doesn’t contain the same color (in step 5) the correct place of this element is also on the bottom layer but instead of being next to the first piece, it is placed diagonally from it. Then we continue with step 5.
  8. After we have our piece inserted into the desired place and correctly rotated, we continue with steps 3-7 until we have all pieces containing white correctly inserted and therefore the “first layer” completed.
  9. If one of our pieces will not be on the upper layer and it will be on the bottom layer (the same on which we build the “first layer”), but rotated incorrectly, we should perform the R U R’ U’ algorithm once – this will pull our element to the top layer. Then we continue with steps 4-8.
